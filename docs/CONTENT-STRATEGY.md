# Content Strategy — Agency AI Employee

## Goal
Use content to validate demand for ClawOps Studio's Agency AI Employee offer, build authority in public, and turn attention into DMs, audit requests, and calls.

This content is not meant to look polished for the sake of looking polished.
It should feel like an agency owner talking to other agency owners who are tired of wasting time on work that should already be automated.

## Audience
Primary audience:
- Digital agency owners
- SaaS founders doing founder-led ops/marketing
- Lean marketing teams

What they care about:
- Saving time without hiring more people
- Protecting margins
- Delivering faster for clients
- Removing repetitive work from the team
- Looking more efficient without increasing headcount

## Content Pillars
Keep it tight. Three pillars max.

### 1. Agency Operator Pain
Show the real work agency owners lose time on:
- onboarding
- reporting
- content workflows
- lead follow-up
- client communication
- task chasing

**Purpose:** make the audience feel seen fast.

### 2. AI Employee Proof
Show what the AI employee actually does in practice:
- 24/7 execution
- workflows automated
- hours saved
- ROI math
- before/after examples
- honest build-in-public lessons

**Purpose:** make the offer concrete and believable.

### 3. Conversion Content
Turn interest into action with low-friction offers:
- free AI audit
- 1-week efficiency audit
- workflow teardown
- DM/call invite
- early adopter positioning

**Purpose:** create conversations and pipeline.

## Tone and Voice
### Voice
- Direct
- Operator-to-operator
- No fluff
- No hypey AI guru language
- Specific numbers over vague claims
- Honest about what works and what still needs improving

### What it should sound like
- "Here's what agency owners are wasting time on."
- "We built this because hiring more people for repetitive work is expensive."
- "If a workflow is predictable, it should not eat senior team time."
- "This won't replace your best people. It removes low-leverage work."

### What it should not sound like
- Corporate SaaS marketing copy
- Buzzword soup
- "Revolutionary cutting-edge transformation"
- Overpromising full replacement of human teams

## Posting Cadence
### Week 1 cadence
- **2 posts per day minimum**
- **14 total posts for the week**
- Primary posting days: Monday to Sunday

### Channel cadence
- **X/Twitter:** daily, fast opinions, threads, behind-the-scenes, punchy ROI takes
- **LinkedIn:** daily or near-daily, sharper POV + founder/operator framing + slightly more structure
- **Facebook Groups / Skool:** 3-4 strong value-first posts per week, written natively and conversationally

### Suggested daily rhythm
- **Morning post:** educational or opinion-led
- **Afternoon post:** proof, offer, or engagement/conversion post

## Platform Role
### X/Twitter
Use for:
- controversial takes
- short operator insights
- threads
- build-in-public
- quick wins and ROI math

### LinkedIn
Use for:
- founder POV
- thoughtful breakdowns
- case-style storytelling
- hiring vs automation comparisons
- audit offers

### Facebook Groups / Skool
Use for:
- practical advice
- questions/polls
- value-first posts
- community discussion
- soft CTA for audits

## Success Metrics
### Primary metrics
- DMs from agency owners
- Comments from qualified operators
- Audit requests
- Calls booked
- Repeat engagement from the same ICP

### Week 1 targets
- **14+ posts published**
- **10+ meaningful conversations** across channels
- **5+ audit requests**
- **2-3 sales calls** attributed to content
- **Top 3 hooks identified** by engagement + replies + DMs

### Quality signals to watch
- People replying with their own workflow pain
- Comments like "we need this" or "how does this work?"
- DMs asking price, setup, or examples
- Saves/shares on workflow posts
- FB/Skool comments from owners asking for audit/help

### What matters less
- Vanity impressions without replies
- Likes from non-buyers
- Broad reach with weak ICP engagement

## Messaging Rules
- Lead with pain, not product features
- Use real numbers whenever possible
- Keep posts publish-ready and founder-voiced
- One clear idea per post
- CTA should be simple: comment, DM, or ask for audit
- 70% value/proof, 30% direct promotion

## CTA Bank
Rotate these naturally:
- "DM me 'audit' and I'll show you where your agency is leaking time."
- "If you run an agency and want me to break down 3 workflows to automate first, comment 'AI'."
- "If you're spending more than 10 hours/week on repeatable ops, this is probably for you."
- "Happy to audit one workflow for free if you want a blunt take."

## Best-Practice Posting Windows
Use **local audience time** rather than your own time zone when scheduling.

- **X:** 8:30-10:00 AM, 12:00-1:30 PM, 5:00-6:30 PM
- **LinkedIn:** 8:00-10:30 AM, 12:00-1:00 PM
- **Facebook Groups / Skool:** 9:00-11:00 AM, 1:00-3:00 PM

## Week 1 Strategy Summary
Week 1 should do three things:
1. Announce the offer clearly
2. Prove the pain is real and expensive
3. Turn attention into audit conversations

If a post gets strong comments or DMs, repurpose it immediately into:
- a follow-up post
- a longer LinkedIn version
- a Facebook/Skool discussion post
- a simple screenshot graphic or carousel
