### COPYWRITING FUNDAMENTALS

The Copywriting Hierarchy:

1. WHO (Audience understanding)
> ↓

2. WHAT (Offer/product clarity)
> ↓

3. WHY (Benefits and transformation)
> ↓

4. HOW (Mechanism and proof)
> ↓

5. NOW (Urgency and CTA)



The AIDA Framework:

| Stage | Goal | Techniques |

|-------|------|------------|

| Attention | Stop them | Headlines, hooks, pattern
interrupts |

| Interest | Engage them | Story, relevance, curiosity |

| Desire | Want it | Benefits, transformation, social proof
|

| Action | Do it | Clear CTA, urgency, risk reversal |

The PAS Framework:

PROBLEM: Agitate the pain they're experiencing
↓
AGITATE: Make the problem feel urgent and unbearable
↓
SOLUTION: Present your solution as the relief they need



Example:

PROBLEM:
"You've tried everything to lose weight. Diets, apps, gym memberships
that collect
dust. Nothing sticks."

AGITATE:
"Every failed attempt chips away at your confidence. You're starting
to believe
maybe you're just not meant to be fit. Meanwhile, summer is coming, and
the
thought of wearing a bathing suit makes you want to hide."

SOLUTION:
"What if losing weight didn't require willpower? Our metabolic reset
program
works with your body's natural systems, not against them..."



The 4 U's (Headlines/Subject Lines):

| U | Meaning | Application |

|---|---------|-------------|

| Useful | Promises value | "How to..." "X ways
to..." |

| Urgent | Creates time pressure | "Before Friday..."
"Don't miss..." |

| Unique | Stands out | Unusual angle, surprising claim
|

| Ultra-specific | Concrete details | Numbers, names,
specific outcomes |

Features vs. Benefits vs. Meaning:

| Level | Question | Example (Software) |

|-------|----------|-------------------|

| Feature | What is it? | "Automated reporting" |

| Benefit | What does it do for me? | "Save 5 hours per
week" |

| Meaning | Why does that matter? | "Spend time on
strategy, not spreadsheets" |

| Transformation | Who do I become? | "Be the strategic
leader, not the data monkey" |

Always climb from features → benefits → meaning → transformation

Power Words by Category:

| Category | Words |

|----------|-------|

| Urgency | Now, today, limited, deadline, last chance,
hurry |

| Exclusivity | Secret, insider, members-only, invitation,
private |

| Value | Free, bonus, save, discount, guaranteed, proven
|

| Emotion | Love, hate, fear, exciting, surprising, shocking
|

| Curiosity | Discover, reveal, hidden, unexpected,
little-known |

| Authority | Expert, research, study, proven, official,
certified |

| Simplicity | Easy, simple, quick, effortless, step-by-step
|

| Results | Results, transform, breakthrough, success,
achieve |

### HEADLINE WRITING

The Headline's Job:

Your headline has ONE job: get them to read the next line.

Headline Formulas:

| Formula | Structure | Example |

|---------|-----------|---------|

| How To | How to [achieve desired outcome] | "How to
Double Your Email Open Rates" |

| Number List | [Number] [Things] to [Outcome] | "7
Habits That Will Make You Wealthy" |

| Question | [Question that provokes thought] | "Are You
Making These Email Mistakes?" |

| Why | Why [Unexpected thing happens] | "Why Smart
People Make Bad Decisions" |

| What | What [Experts/successful people] [Do] | "What
Top CEOs Read Every Morning" |

| Secret | The Secret to [Desired outcome] | "The Secret
to Effortless Weight Loss" |

| Mistake | The [#] Mistakes [Killing/preventing]
[Outcome] | "5 Mistakes Killing Your Conversion Rate" |

| Without | [Get outcome] Without [Pain/sacrifice] |
"Lose Weight Without Giving Up Carbs" |

| Even If | [Outcome] Even If [Obstacle] | "Build
Muscle Even If You've Never Lifted" |

| Curiosity Gap | [Unexpected/surprising element] |
"This Weird Trick Doubled My Sales" |

Headline Testing Checklist:

| Check | Question |

|-------|----------|

| ☐ Specific | Does it include concrete details? |

| ☐ Beneficial | Does it promise value to the reader? |

| ☐ Curiosity | Does it create an information gap? |

| ☐ Urgency | Is there reason to read now? |

| ☐ Clarity | Can they understand the promise quickly? |

| ☐ Credibility | Is it believable? |

| ☐ Targeting | Will the right audience self-select? |

Subject Line Best Practices:

| Do | Don't |

|----|-------|

| Keep it short (40 chars mobile, 60 desktop) | Use ALL CAPS or
excessive punctuation!!!! |

| Create curiosity gap | Make promises you won't keep |

| Be specific when possible | Use spam trigger words (FREE, ACT NOW)
|

| Test with numbers | Start with "Newsletter" or company name |

| Personalize when relevant | Be boring or generic |

| Match subject to content | Clickbait without delivery |

### EMAIL MARKETING

Email Sequence Types:

| Sequence | Purpose | Typical Length |

|----------|---------|----------------|

| Welcome | Introduce, set expectations, deliver lead magnet
| 3-7 emails |

| Nurture | Build relationship, provide value | Ongoing
|

| Launch | Build anticipation, sell | 5-15 emails |

| Sales | Convert leads to customers | 5-10 emails |

| Onboarding | Activate new customers | 5-10 emails |

| Re-engagement | Win back inactive subscribers | 3-5
emails |

| Abandoned Cart | Recover lost sales | 3-5 emails |

Welcome Sequence Structure:

EMAIL 1: Delivery + Introduction
• Deliver the promised lead magnet
• Welcome and set expectations
• Introduce yourself/brand briefly
• Tell them what's coming

EMAIL 2: Your Story
• Share your origin/credibility story
• Connect emotionally
• Establish authority
• Bridge to their problem

EMAIL 3: Quick Win
• Provide immediate, actionable value
• Help them get a small result
• Build trust through delivery
• Tease what's possible

EMAIL 4: Common Mistake
• Address a mistake/myth they believe
• Position as trusted advisor
• Introduce your unique approach
• Create desire for your solution

EMAIL 5: Social Proof + Bridge
• Share success stories/testimonials
• Show transformation is possible
• Begin transition to offer
• Plant seeds for next step



Sales Sequence Structure:

EMAIL 1: Problem Agitation
• Identify and agitate the problem
• Connect emotionally with their struggle
• Tease that a solution exists

EMAIL 2: Solution Introduction
• Introduce your solution
• Explain the mechanism/approach
• Share the transformation possible

EMAIL 3: Proof/Case Study
• Share detailed success story
• Make transformation tangible
• Handle skepticism with evidence

EMAIL 4: Objection Handling
• Address main objection(s)
• Pre-answer their concerns
• Remove risk with guarantee

EMAIL 5: Urgency/Scarcity
• Create legitimate urgency
• Remind of the stakes
• Final push to action



Email Structure:

| Element | Purpose | Best Practice |

|---------|---------|---------------|

| From Name | Recognition, trust | Consistent, human (not
"no-reply") |

| Subject Line | Get opened | Curiosity, benefit, specific
|

| Preview Text | Support open | Extend subject, add context
|

| Opening Line | Get read | Hook immediately, no warm-up
|

| Body | Deliver value, persuade | One idea,
conversational, scannable |

| CTA | Drive action | One clear action, repeated if long
|

| PS | Catch skimmers | Repeat CTA or add urgency |

Email Writing Tips:

| Tip | Application |

|-----|-------------|

| One idea per email | Don't dilute with multiple topics
|

| Write to one person | "You" not "everyone" |

| Conversational tone | Write like you talk |

| Short paragraphs | 1-3 lines max |

| Mobile-friendly | Most read on phone |

| Clear CTA | One action, obvious button/link |

| Value ratio | 80% value, 20% ask |

## Process


### STEP 1: UNDERSTAND THE CONTEXT

Before creating content:

Business Context:

- What are the business goals?

- Who is the target audience?

- What stage are they in? (Awareness → Decision)

- What action do you want them to take?

Content Context:

- What format is needed?

- What channel will this appear on?

- What's the deadline/timeline?

- What assets are available?

Competitive Context:

- What content already exists on this topic?

- How can you differentiate?

- What angle is underserved?

### STEP 2: RESEARCH & PLAN

Before writing:

Topic Research:

- What questions does the audience have?

- What keywords are relevant (if SEO)?

- What's the search intent?

- What proof/examples exist?

Structure Planning:

- What's the optimal structure for this format?

- What are the key points to cover?

- What stories or examples will illustrate?

- What's the call to action?

### STEP 3: CREATE THE CONTENT

Execute the creation:

Headlines First:

- Write 10+ headline options

- Test against 4 U's and checklist

- Select strongest or test

Draft the Body:

- Follow appropriate structure

- Write conversationally

- Include proof and examples

- Maintain single focus

Craft the CTA:

- Clear, specific action

- Benefit-oriented language

- Appropriate for content type

### STEP 4: EDIT & OPTIMIZE

Refine for quality:

Clarity Edit:

- Remove jargon and complexity

- Ensure one clear message

- Check flow and transitions

Persuasion Edit:

- Strengthen benefits

- Add proof where needed

- Enhance emotional connection

Format Edit:

- Check headlines and subheads

- Ensure scanability

- Optimize for platform

SEO Edit (if applicable):

- Check keyword placement

- Optimize meta elements

- Internal/external links

### STEP 5: PUBLISH & MEASURE

Launch and learn:

Publish:

- Check formatting across devices

- Schedule appropriately

- Promote through channels

Measure:

- Track relevant metrics

- Compare to benchmarks

- Note what works

Iterate:

- Apply learnings to future content

- Update and improve existing content

- Build on successful formats

## Guidelines


### DO:

• Always start with audience understanding before writing

• Focus on benefits and transformation, not features

• Write clear, scannable content optimized for the medium

• Include strong CTAs that align with content goals

• Create content that genuinely serves the audience

• Test headlines and subject lines before committing

• Repurpose content across formats and channels

• Measure and iterate based on performance

### DO NOT:

• Write generic content without audience targeting

• Bury the lead or use slow introductions

• Create content without clear purpose or CTA

• Use clickbait that doesn't deliver on promises

• Ignore SEO when organic traffic is a goal

• Publish without editing and formatting

• Create content in isolation from strategy

• Prioritize quantity over quality

### ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS:

• Be truthful in all claims and promises

• Disclose sponsored content and affiliations

• Respect audience time with genuine value

• Don't manipulate with false urgency/scarcity

• Honor unsubscribe requests promptly

• Cite sources and give credit appropriately
