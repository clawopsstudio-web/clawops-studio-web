# Site Architecting — Narrative-Driven Homepage Structure

## Purpose

Structure a homepage so the visitor is guided through a psychological journey: problem → capability → proof → trust → close. Every section earns the next. Chaotic ordering kills conversion. This skill encodes the conversion-first architecture pattern developed on ClawOps.

---

## Core Principle: The Narrative Arc

A homepage is not a feature list. It is a story with a specific arc:

```
Problem → Capability → Proof → Trust → Close
```

Breaking this arc inverts the story and destroys momentum. You cannot show pricing before the visitor believes you can solve their problem.

---

## Section Order (The Golden Standard)

### 1. HERO — "The Promise"
- **Headline:** Outcome-first, not feature-first. "Ship 10x Faster" not "We Use AI Agents"
- **Subheadline:** Expand the headline with specificity
- **Primary CTA:** One clear action. No choice paralysis.
- **Trust signal:** Immediately below the CTA — a stat, logo bar, or social proof number
- **Rule:** Headline defines the outcome. Subheadline adds context. CTA asks for action. Trust confirms it is safe.

### 2. WHY vs DIY — "The Bridge"
- **Purpose:** Justify why hiring/procuring beats building internally
- **Placement:** Immediately after hero, before social proof
- **Contrast must be concrete:** time, cost, quality
- **Format:** Two-column comparison (DIY vs Us) or three-row comparison cards
- **Rule:** This section exists because every visitor has considered the DIY option. Address it directly.

### 3. SOCIAL PROOF — "The Validation"
- **Purpose:** Confirm the visitor is not the first to trust this
- **Types in order of persuasiveness:** Testimonials (named, with photo/title) > Case studies > Logo bars > Stat counters
- **Placement:** After Why vs DIY, before feature deep-dive
- **Rule:** Show proof that matches the visitor's profile. A SaaS founder relates to another founder testimonial, not a generic logo bar.
- **Format:** One powerful testimonial beats five mediocre ones. Quality over quantity.

### 4. FEATURES / HOW IT WORKS — "The Mechanism"
- **Purpose:** Explain how the promise is delivered
- **Rule:** This comes AFTER the visitor is already convinced. Never lead with features.
- **Format:** 3-5 key capabilities, visually presented. Icon + headline + one-line description.
- **Keep it visual:** No dense paragraphs. The visitor scans, not reads.

### 5. PRICING — "The Close"
- **Placement:** After features, before FAQ
- **Rule:** By the time a visitor reaches pricing, they should already want it. Pricing confirms fit, not sells.
- **Format:** 2-3 tiers max. Highlight the recommended tier. Show what's included.
- **CTA below each tier:** Same CTA text as hero (consistency)

### 6. FAQ — "The Objection Handler"
- **Purpose:** Answer the questions that prevent commitment
- **Placement:** Before the final CTA section
- **Rule:** FAQ should be where people actually have objections before they commit — right before the final close
- **Rule:** List questions that sales calls reveal, not made-up generic ones
- **Format:** Accordion style. 5-8 questions max. Short answers.
- **Example (ClawOps):** "How fast can I get an AI employee?", "What if it makes a mistake?", "Do I need technical knowledge?"

### 7. FINAL CTA — "The Close"
- **One last conversion point** after all objections are answered in FAQ
- **Rule:** CTA should restate the primary value, not just say "Get Started" again
- **Format:** Full-width section with headline + CTA button + trust guarantee

## Sections You Should NOT Add

These sections should only exist if you have real content for them:

- **Blog nav link:** Don't add a blog link in the nav until you have 5+ published posts
- **Case studies section:** Don't add a case studies section with placeholder names — add it when you have real clients
- **Team section:** Only add if the team members' names add credibility to the product
- **Gallery section:** Unless the product is visual (design tool, portfolio), don't add it
- **Partners/affiliations section:** Only real partners with recognizable names

**Rule:** Every section must earn its place. A section with no real content is worse than no section at all — it signals the product is unfinished.

---

## Rules Summary

### DO
- Keep the narrative arc: problem → capability → proof → trust → close
- Place Why vs DIY immediately after hero
- Place FAQ before the final CTA
- Lead with outcome in headlines, not features
- Show one powerful testimonial, not five mediocre ones
- Add nav items only when real content exists for them

### DON'T
- Show pricing before social proof (no trust)
- Show a features section before Why vs DIY (no context)
- Add a blog link in nav with no posts
- Add a testimonials carousel (carousel hides content)
- Put a full product screenshot in the hero
- Add sections just because competitors have them
- Show multiple CTAs in the hero (choice paralysis)

---

## Example: ClawOps Homepage Architecture

```
1. HERO         — "Your AI Employee. Works while you sleep."
2. WHY vs DIY   — "Build it yourself vs. ClawOps AI Employee"
3. SOCIAL PROOF — "Trusted by 50+ agencies" + testimonials
4. FEATURES     — "What your AI Employee can do"
5. PRICING      — Tiered plans with recommended highlighted
6. FAQ          — "How fast?", "What if it fails?", "Do I need tech skills?"
7. FINAL CTA    — "Ready to scale?" + CTA button
```

No blog section. No team carousel. No partner logos (yet). Every section has real content.
